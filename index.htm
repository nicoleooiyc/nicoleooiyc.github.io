What Is Mental Health?

Mental health is the emotional, psychological, and social well-being that impacts how you think, feel, and act. It enables you to cope with life's stresses, realize your potential, learn and work well, and contribute to your community. Good mental health is more than just the absence of a mental illness and is crucial for your overall quality of life.

Common Mental Health Challenges

Nearly 1 in 7 people worldwide live with a mental disorder, which can take many different forms and, if left untreated, may severely disrupt daily life and cause long-term suffering; although effective prevention and treatment options exist, most people still do not have access to the care they need.

According to WHO (World Health Organization), in 2021 nearly 1 in every 7 people (1.1 billion) around the world were living with a mental disorder, with anxiety and depressive disorders the most common (1). While effective prevention and treatment options exist, most people with mental disorders do not have access to effective care. Many people also experience stigma, discrimination and violations of human rights.


Examples of common mental health challenges includes...

Stress

Often appears as the body‚Äôs natural response to pressure, developing when school, work, relationships, or personal expectations create demands that feel too heavy to manage, causing both emotional tension and physical strain.

Anxiety

Involves persistent worry and physical tension that can interfere with normal activities, causing feelings of restlessness, irritability, a constant sense of unease, and often leading to difficulties in concentrating, completing work or school tasks, and maintaining social relationships.

Depression

Diminish mood, energy, and motivation, often causing persistent sadness, fatigue, and a sense of hopelessness, which can make even simple daily tasks such as getting out of bed, preparing meals, or completing schoolwork feel overwhelming and difficult to manage.

Burnout

Develops after long periods of chronic stress, gradually eroding emotional resilience and mental stamina, and ultimately leading to profound emotional exhaustion, a sense of detachment from work or personal life, and decreased engagement in activities that once felt meaningful or enjoyable.

Eating disorders

Disrupts healthy attitudes toward food and body image, often leading to serious physical and emotional consequences, as well as challenges in daily functioning and social relationships.

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)

Can be develop after experiencing or witnessing traumatic events, causing persistent anxiety, intrusive memories, emotional distress, and difficulties in maintaining normal routines or relationships.

How to Improve Mental Well-Being

There are many methods to maintaining mental well-being involves developing consistent habits and strategies that support emotional balance, reduce stress, and improve overall one's quality of life.

Examples of methods to impove mental well-being includes...

Healthy Daily Habits

Maintaining a regular routine that includes sufficient sleep, balanced meals, and consistent self-care can strengthen both physical and mental health, helping to regulate mood and energy throughout the day.

Mindfulness

Practicing mindfulness through meditation, deep breathing exercises, or quiet reflection allows the mind to slow down, reduces anxiety, and fosters a sense of presence and calm even during stressful situations.

Time Management

Organizing tasks, setting realistic goals, and breaking work into manageable steps can prevent overwhelm, reduce stress, and increase productivity in both personal and academic life.

Talk to Someone

Sharing thoughts, concerns, or emotions with a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional provides perspective, emotional relief, and practical support for navigating challenges.

Physical Activity

Regular movement or exercise, such as walking, yoga, or team sports, not only improves physical health but also releases endorphins that boost mood and reduce stress.

Engage in Relaxing Hobbies

Spending time on hobbies or creative activities, such as reading, drawing, cooking, or gardening, provides a healthy distraction from stress, encourages mindfulness, and promotes a sense of accomplishment and personal fulfillment.
